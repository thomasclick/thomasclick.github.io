Building an Off-YouTube Community with Discord or Facebook Groups

A guide to creating a dedicated space for your most loyal fans to connect with you and each other.

Introduction: Why Move a Community Off-YouTube?

As a YouTube creator, you know that engagement is everything. While the YouTube comment section is a start, building an off-YouTube community on a platform like Discord or a Facebook Group allows you to create a deeper, more meaningful connection with your audience. This dedicated space transforms passive viewers into a loyal tribe, fostering an environment where your most dedicated fans can interact, share ideas, and feel like part of something special. This guide will walk you through the process of creating and nurturing a thriving community hub that supports your channel's growth.

Definition: What is an Off-YouTube Community?

An off-YouTube community is a dedicated, private or public space hosted on a third-party platform where your subscribers can gather. Unlike the transient nature of YouTube comments, these platforms offer persistent chat, organized discussions, direct messaging, and rich media sharing. The two most popular choices for creators are Discord servers and Facebook Groups, each offering a unique set of features tailored to different community styles.

Option 1: Creating a Discord Server

  1. Create Your Server: Download the Discord app or use the web version. Click the '+' icon on the server list and select 'Create My Own' followed by the 'For a club or community' template.
  2. Set Up Channels: Create text channels for different topics (e.g., #general-chat, #video-ideas, #announcements, #memes) and voice channels for live chats or gaming sessions.
  3. Establish Rules & Roles: Create a #rules channel. Set up roles (e.g., Moderator, Super Fan) to grant permissions and reward active members.
  4. Invite Members: Create a permanent invite link and promote it in your YouTube video descriptions, end screens, and community tab posts.

Option 2: Creating a Facebook Group

  1. Create Your Group: From your Facebook homepage, click 'Groups' in the left menu, then 'Create New Group'. Give it a name related to your channel.
  2. Choose Privacy Settings: Decide between a 'Public' group (anyone can see and join) or a 'Private' group (members must be approved), which is often better for fostering a safe, exclusive environment.
  3. Customize Your Group: Add a cover photo that matches your channel's branding and write a clear description of the group's purpose and rules.
  4. Promote the Group: Share the link to your group on your YouTube channel and other social media profiles. Use the 'ask questions' feature for new members to screen entrants.

Advantages and Outcomes of a Dedicated Community

  • Deeper Fan Connection: Engage in real-time conversations, get to know your followers on a personal level, and build genuine relationships.
  • Direct Feedback Loop: Instantly gather ideas, poll your audience on future content, and get constructive feedback to improve your videos.
  • Increased Loyalty & Retention: A strong community makes fans feel valued, increasing the likelihood they will consistently watch your content and support your channel long-term.
  • Monetization Opportunities: Offer exclusive content, early access, or paid tiers through platforms like Discord, creating a new revenue stream.
  • Content Amplification: Your community becomes a team of advocates who are first to like, comment on, and share your new videos, boosting initial performance.
