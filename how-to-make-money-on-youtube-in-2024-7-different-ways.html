How to Make Money on YouTube in 2024 (7 Different Ways)

Turning a YouTube channel into a source of income is a dream for many creators. In 2024, the opportunities to make money on YouTube have expanded far beyond simple ad revenue. This guide will walk you through seven different, effective ways to monetize your content, from traditional methods like the YouTube Partner Program to more entrepreneurial ventures like selling merchandise and securing brand deals. Whether you're a new creator or an established one, understanding these monetization strategies is the first step toward building a sustainable career on the platform.

7 Proven Methods for YouTube Monetization in 2024

1. The YouTube Partner Program (YPP) & AdSense

This is the most well-known method. Once you're accepted into the YPP, YouTube places ads on your videos, and you earn a portion of the revenue generated. It’s the foundational layer of monetization for most YouTubers.

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Meet Eligibility Requirements: You need at least 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 valid public watch hours in the last 12 months (or 10 million valid public Shorts views in the last 90 days).
  2. Apply: Once you meet the threshold, you can apply for the YPP through the 'Earn' tab in YouTube Studio.
  3. Get Reviewed: YouTube will review your channel to ensure it complies with their monetization policies.
  4. Set Up AdSense: Link an approved AdSense account to receive payments.

Advantages and Outcomes:

This method provides a relatively passive income stream that grows as your channel's viewership increases. To accelerate your journey to 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 watch hours, consider using growth tools. An app like Tube Boost can help you connect with other creators and organically grow your audience to meet these requirements faster.

2. Affiliate Marketing

Affiliate marketing involves promoting other companies' products or services. You include special tracking links in your video descriptions, and when a viewer makes a purchase through your link, you earn a commission.

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Find a Niche: Focus on products that are relevant to your content and audience.
  2. Join Affiliate Programs: Sign up for programs like Amazon Associates, ShareASale, or directly with brands you love.
  3. Create Authentic Content: Make genuine product reviews, tutorials, or 'favorites' videos. Don't just sell; provide value.
  4. Disclose Your Links: Always be transparent with your audience that you are using affiliate links.

Relevant Tools and Resources:

  • Amazon Associates: Ideal for a wide range of products.
  • ShareASale & Rakuten Advertising: Access to thousands of merchants.
  • Kit.co: A great way to organize and share your favorite gear.

3. Selling Your Own Merchandise

Selling branded merchandise—like t-shirts, mugs, and hats—is a fantastic way to build a deeper connection with your community while opening a new revenue stream. YouTube's Merch Shelf integration makes this seamless.

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Design Your Merch: Create designs that resonate with your brand and inside jokes from your channel.
  2. Choose a Platform: Use a print-on-demand service like Spring (formerly Teespring) or Spreadshop. They handle printing and shipping.
  3. Enable the Merch Shelf: If you're eligible (part of YPP with over 10k subs), you can connect your store to display products directly below your videos.
  4. Promote: Wear your merch in videos and actively promote it to your audience.

4. Brand Deals and Sponsorships

Sponsorships are one of the most lucrative ways to make money on YouTube. Brands will pay you to feature, review, or mention their product in a dedicated video or through an integrated segment.

Workflow Overview:

To attract high-paying sponsors, brands look for channels with strong engagement and a loyal following. Building a professional brand presence is critical. Growth-focused tools like Tube Boost can help you increase your channel's metrics, making it more attractive to potential brand partners.

5. Fan Funding Features

YouTube offers built-in tools that allow your most loyal fans to support you directly. These include Channel Memberships (monthly subscriptions for perks), Super Chat & Super Stickers (paid comments during live streams), and Super Thanks (tips on uploaded videos).

Advantages and Outcomes:

Fan funding creates a strong community and provides a more predictable income source, especially with monthly memberships. It's a direct way for your audience to show their appreciation.

6. Selling Digital Products or Courses

If you have expertise in a particular area, you can package that knowledge into a digital product. This could be an ebook, a video course, presets, templates, or software. You use your YouTube channel as the primary marketing tool to drive sales.

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Identify a Problem: What do your viewers constantly ask about? Find a problem you can solve.
  2. Create a High-Value Product: Develop a comprehensive course, ebook, or resource.
  3. Host Your Product: Use platforms like Gumroad, Podia, or Teachable.
  4. Promote via Your Content: Create videos that offer free value related to your product and direct viewers to the sales page for the full solution.

7. Licensing Your Viral Content

If you capture a unique, newsworthy, or viral-worthy moment on camera, media companies, ad agencies, and TV shows might want to license it from you. Instead of them using it for free, you get paid for the rights.

Advantages and Outcomes:

This can result in a sudden, significant payout from a single video. If a video of yours starts gaining unexpected traction, be proactive about managing its rights.
