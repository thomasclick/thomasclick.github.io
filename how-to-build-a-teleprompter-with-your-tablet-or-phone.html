How to Build a Teleprompter with Your Tablet or Phone

A DIY guide to creating a cheap and effective teleprompter setup using devices you already own.

Introduction: Speak Like a Pro on Camera

Struggling to remember your lines or constantly looking away from the camera? Learning how to build a teleprompter with your tablet or phone is the perfect, low-cost solution. This simple DIY project can dramatically improve the quality of your video content by allowing you to maintain perfect eye contact with your audience while reading your script. This guide provides a step-by-step walkthrough to create a cheap, homemade teleprompter setup that rivals expensive professional equipment.

What is a Teleprompter and Why Do You Need One?

A teleprompter, or autocue, is a device that displays scrolling text in front of a camera lens. This allows a speaker to read their script while looking directly into the camera. For video creators, this means no more awkward pauses, forgotten points, or shifting eyes. The result is a more confident, professional, and engaging delivery.

Step-by-Step Guide: Building Your DIY Teleprompter

Follow these simple steps to build your own teleprompter in under an hour.

Materials Needed:

  • A cardboard box (slightly wider than your tablet/phone).
  • Your tablet or smartphone.
  • A piece of clear glass or acrylic (plexiglass) cut to fit the box's dimensions.
  • A dark, non-reflective piece of fabric (like a black t-shirt or felt).
  • A utility knife or box cutter.
  • Duct tape or a hot glue gun.
  • A teleprompter app for your device.

Assembly Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Box: Cut an opening on the top of the box large enough for your tablet or phone to lie flat, screen facing up. On the front-facing side of the box, cut a small, circular hole just big enough for your camera lens to see through.
  2. Create the Reflector Slot: Cut a slit across the top of the box at a 45-degree angle relative to the base. This is where your glass or acrylic panel will slide in. The angle is crucial for reflecting the text from your device's screen towards you.
  3. Insert the Glass: Carefully slide the glass or acrylic panel into the 45-degree slot. Secure it with tape or glue if necessary. This panel will reflect the script from your tablet into your line of sight.
  4. Position Your Devices: Place your tablet or phone flat on the opening you created, with the screen facing the reflective glass. Place your camera behind the box, aligning its lens with the hole you cut.
  5. Block Reflections: Drape the dark fabric over the camera and the back of the box to create a 'light hood.' This prevents unwanted light and reflections from appearing on the glass and in your video.

Advantages and Outcomes of a DIY Setup

Using a homemade teleprompter offers several incredible benefits:

  • Cost-Effective: Save hundreds of dollars compared to buying a commercial teleprompter.
  • Professionalism: Your delivery will be smoother, and direct eye contact builds trust and connection with your audience.
  • Efficiency: Reduce the number of takes and cut down on editing time significantly.
  • Versatility: Perfect for YouTube videos, online courses, corporate presentations, social media updates, and more.
