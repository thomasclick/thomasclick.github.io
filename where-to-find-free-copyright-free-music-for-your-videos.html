What is Copyright-Free & Royalty-Free Music?

Understanding the terminology is the first step to staying safe:

  • Copyright-Free Music: This term often refers to music in the Public Domain. The copyright has expired, been forfeited, or was never applicable, making it free for anyone to use without permission.
  • Royalty-Free Music: This is a common misconception. It doesn't mean the music is free. It means you pay a one-time fee (or get it for free from certain sites) to acquire a license to use the music as many times as you want without paying additional royalties for each play.
  • Creative Commons (CC) Music: This music is free to use, but you must follow the specific rules of its license. Most commonly, this involves giving attribution (credit) to the original artist. Always check the license type (e.g., CC BY, CC BY-SA).

Top Resources for Free, Safe Music

Here is a list of the best platforms to find high-quality, royalty-free, and Creative Commons music for your videos.

1. YouTube Audio Library

Directly integrated into YouTube Studio, this is the safest and easiest option for YouTubers. All tracks are pre-cleared for use on the platform.

  • Pros: Completely free, no copyright issues on YouTube, easy to search and filter.
  • Cons: Music can be generic and overused by other creators.
  • Link: Accessible directly from your YouTube Studio dashboard.

2. Pixabay Music

Pixabay offers a vast library of royalty-free music that is safe to use across multiple platforms without attribution, though checking each track's license is still good practice.

  • Pros: High-quality tracks, no attribution required for most songs, easy-to-use interface.
  • Cons: Popular tracks can be found in many other videos.
  • Link: pixabay.com/music

3. Uppbeat

Uppbeat offers a freemium model with a great selection of high-quality music from talented artists. Free users can download a limited number of tracks per month.

  • Pros: Fresh, modern music. Simple crediting system to avoid copyright claims.
  • Cons: The free plan has download limits.
  • Link: uppbeat.io

4. Free Music Archive (FMA)

A massive library of music with various Creative Commons licenses. It's a treasure trove for unique sounds, but you must pay close attention to the license requirements for each track.

  • Pros: Huge and diverse selection of music. Great for finding unique tracks.
  • Cons: You must carefully check and comply with each song's specific CC license.
  • Link: freemusicarchive.org
